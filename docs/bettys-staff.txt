Staff: Meet Our Flock! 

James - Owner 

James purchased Betty's Bird Boutique in 2025 after a long career in finance. He'll be the first to 
admit that he didn't know the first thing about birds when he took over the store. In fact, he was 
so clueless that he once tried to give a cockatoo a carrot stick with ranch dressing on it. Despite 
his learning curve, his love for the store and its mission is as strong as his desire to provide the 
best for his new friends. James has dedicated himself to learning everything he can about avian 
care and has been known to spend hours watching documentaries and reading books about 
bird behavior. He now owns his own feathery friend, a charming green-cheeked conure named 
Sheldon. 

He's currently in the process of building an extensive bird library at home, much to the 
amusement of his family. He's also trying to perfect his whistle to be able to communicate with 
Sheldon, though so far he's only managed to teach him to make the sound of a ringing phone. 

Maria - Store Manager 

Maria has been with Betty's Bird Boutique for over 15 years, starting as a part-time sales 
associate in high school. Her passion for birds is infectious, and she has a knack for helping 
customers find the perfect toy or food for their companions. Maria is a proud parront to a flock of 
her own, including two boisterous African grey parrots named Apollo and Artemis, and a shy 
Quaker parrot named Olive. She's an expert in bird nutrition and loves to share her knowledge 
of foraging and creating enriching environments. 

Maria's favorite bird fact is that hummingbirds can fly backward. She also has a talent for 
mimicking the specific calls of various bird species, which she uses to greet the store's regular 
avian visitors. 

David - Head of Inventory 

David, a lifelong bird lover, joined the team five years ago after volunteering at a local bird 
sanctuary. His expertise in identifying different bird species by their calls is legendary. He can 
often be found in the back room, humming along to the songs of the store's various occupants. 
David owns a beautiful sun conure named Mango, who is known for his mischievous personality 
and his love of bell peppers. 

A fun fact about David is that he once successfully taught Mango to say "I am Groot," and he 
often jokes that Mango has a better grasp of the line than he does. 

Chloe - Avian Specialist 

Chloe is the newest member of the team, having recently graduated with a degree in 
ornithology. Her deep understanding of bird behavior and health has made her an invaluable 
resource for both staff and customers. Chloe is passionate about avian enrichment and loves 
helping customers design stimulating play areas for their birds. She owns a stunning cockatiel 
named Pip, who is a master of the wolf whistle. 

Chloe's favorite bird-related fun fact is that the fastest bird in the world is the peregrine falcon, 
which can reach speeds of over 200 miles per hour during its hunting dive. 

Leo - The Store Mascot 

Leo, a Moluccan cockatoo, is the unofficial head of customer relations. He was adopted by Betty 
Winger herself after his previous owners could no longer care for him. Leo is now a permanent 
resident of Betty's Bird Boutique, where he has his very own elaborate cage and a special diet 
of gourmet seeds and fresh fruit. He loves to chat with customers, often greeting them with a 
cheerful "Hello!" and a head bob. He also loves to show off his dance moves whenever music is 
playing. 

Leo has a particular fondness for new people and loves to lean forward and offer a wing to 
shake, a trick he learned from Betty Winger herself. His favorite treats are almonds, which he 
will happily accept in exchange for a friendly "Goodbye!" 

Sarah - Sales Associate 

Sarah is a kind and patient sales associate who is known for her exceptional customer service. 
She owns a pair of adorable zebra finches named Finn and Feathers. Sarah loves helping new 
bird owners feel confident in caring for their new pets. She has a talent for explaining complex 
topics, like molting and feather health, in a way that's easy to understand. 

A fun fact about Sarah is that she knows the sound of every single bird in her local park and can 
identify them just by listening to their calls.
 
Benjamin - Sales Associate 

Benjamin's enthusiasm for birds is contagious. He is the go-to person for all things toy-related. 
He has a talent for creating unique, safe toys out of everyday materials. Benjamin owns a 
charming budgie named Mochi and a sweet cockatiel named Sesame. He loves nothing more 
than to help customers pick out the perfect new toy to keep their feathered friends entertained. 

He once taught Mochi to say "I love you," and he considers it one of his greatest 
accomplishments.
